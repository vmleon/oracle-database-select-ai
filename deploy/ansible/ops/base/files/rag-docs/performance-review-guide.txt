PERFORMANCE REVIEW GUIDE
Acme Corporation

REVIEW CYCLE

Acme Corporation conducts formal performance reviews twice per year:
- Mid-year review: June (lightweight check-in, no rating)
- Annual review: December (full review with rating and compensation discussion)

The annual review cycle timeline:
- November 1-15: Self-assessments due
- November 15-30: Manager assessments and calibration
- December 1-10: Calibration meetings across departments
- December 10-20: Review conversations between manager and employee
- January: Compensation adjustments effective

RATING SCALE

Employees are rated on a 5-point scale:

5 - Exceptional: Consistently exceeds all expectations. Delivers outstanding results and is a role model for others. Reserved for truly extraordinary performance (top 5%).

4 - Exceeds Expectations: Frequently exceeds expectations in most areas. Delivers strong results and demonstrates growth. Approximately 20% of employees.

3 - Meets Expectations: Consistently meets all expectations and occasionally exceeds them. Solid, reliable performance that fully satisfies role requirements. Approximately 50% of employees.

2 - Needs Improvement: Meets some expectations but falls short in key areas. Requires a development plan with specific milestones. Approximately 20% of employees.

1 - Unsatisfactory: Does not meet minimum expectations. Requires immediate performance improvement plan (PIP) with clear consequences. Approximately 5% of employees.

EVALUATION CRITERIA

Performance is assessed across four dimensions:

1. Results and Impact (40% weight)
   - Achievement of goals and objectives
   - Quality and timeliness of deliverables
   - Business impact and value created

2. Skills and Expertise (25% weight)
   - Technical or functional competence
   - Problem-solving and decision-making
   - Continuous learning and skill development

3. Collaboration and Communication (20% weight)
   - Teamwork and cross-functional partnership
   - Communication clarity and effectiveness
   - Mentoring and knowledge sharing

4. Leadership and Initiative (15% weight)
   - Proactive identification of opportunities or issues
   - Influence and leadership (regardless of title)
   - Alignment with company values

GOAL SETTING

Employees and managers collaborate on setting 3-5 goals each review cycle using the SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). Goals should include:
- At least 1 business/results goal
- At least 1 development/growth goal
- Goals aligned with team and company objectives

Goals are documented in the HR portal and reviewed during monthly 1:1 meetings.

PROMOTION PROCESS

Promotions are considered during the annual review cycle. Criteria include:
- Consistent performance rating of 4 or above for at least 2 consecutive cycles
- Demonstration of skills at the next level for at least 6 months
- Manager nomination and skip-level approval
- Budget availability within the department
- Minimum 12 months in current role

Promotion decisions are finalized during December calibration meetings. Off-cycle promotions are rare and require VP approval with HR partnership.

COMPENSATION ADJUSTMENTS

Annual merit increases are tied to performance ratings:
- Rating 5: 6-10% increase
- Rating 4: 4-6% increase
- Rating 3: 2-4% increase
- Rating 2: 0-1% increase
- Rating 1: No increase

Market adjustments may be applied independently of performance ratings to ensure competitive compensation.

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLANS (PIP)

Employees rated 1 or 2 may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan:
- Duration: 60-90 days
- Includes specific, measurable improvement targets
- Weekly check-ins with manager
- HR Business Partner involvement
- Successful completion returns the employee to regular review process
- Failure to meet PIP objectives may result in termination

SELF-ASSESSMENT TIPS

- Be specific: use data, metrics, and concrete examples
- Reference your goals and how you achieved or progressed toward them
- Highlight accomplishments and their business impact
- Acknowledge areas for growth honestly
- Document feedback received from peers and stakeholders
- Propose development goals for the next cycle
